North Carolina Animal Advocates United (NCAAU)

We are a grassroots, loosely organized group of activists interested in furthering animal rights in North Carolina. By "animal rights" we mean anything that reduces the suffering and exploitation of all non-human animals. While we support animal liberation in its entirety, we understand and support welfare initiatives that help meet our goal. As a collective that's loosely organized, we can align ourselves with any animal rights work orchestrated by another organization or individual. We are also empowered to pursue our own actions and activism as we see fit. All campaigns and actions are encouraged so long as they adhere to our core values and code of conduct.

Initiatives

Vegan Outreach

Vegan outreach invites the public into thoughtful, non-judgmental conversations while offering free plant-based food as a welcoming entry point. Paired with engaging brochures and literature, it turns a simple interaction into an accessible, positive introduction to compassionate living.

Puppy Mill Protests

The commercial dog breeding industry treats dogs as commodities rather than individuals, prioritizing profit over health, wellbeing, and ethical care. Puppy mills are especially notorious for overcrowding, neglect, genetic health problems, and poor living conditions, while continued breeding contributes to overpopulation when millions of dogs already sit in shelters or are euthanized each year.

Cage-Free Campaigns

When companies publicly commit to going cage-free but fail to honor those promises, they betray both animal welfare and consumer trust. Activists respond by holding them accountable through campaigns and protests that expose the gap between corporate claims and real action, pushing brands to finally follow through.

Cube of Truth

The Vegan Cube of Truth is a powerful form of street outreach where activists stand in silent formation, holding screens that reveal the realities of animal exploitation to passersby. By combining striking visuals with calm, compassionate conversation, it creates a moment of undeniable awareness that often sparks deep reflection and meaningful change.

Foie Gras Free Triangle

Foie gras is produced by violently force-feeding ducks or geese until their livers become grotesquely enlarged, causing intense suffering, organ failure, and often death solely for human indulgence. NCAAU is demanding all restaurants in the Triangle stop selling foie gras.

Chalktivism

Vegan chalktivism is a creative form of grassroots activism that uses washable chalk to place compelling vegan and animal-rights messages on sidewalks and other paved surfaces, meeting people in their everyday paths. By transforming ordinary public spaces into moments of reflection and curiosity, chalktivism spreads the message in an approachable, eye-catching, and accessible way.

Virtual Activism

Virtual vegan activism empowers people to create real change for animals from anywhere by using digital tools like social media, petitions, and online campaigns. It turns everyday clicks, posts, and messages into coordinated action that can shift culture, pressure corporations, and influence policy at scale.
